Quantitative anthropology at Purdue

The QuantLab brings archaeology, human evolution, anthropological theory, and quantitative modeling into the same workspace. The lab links data science, simulation, AI, morphometrics, and statistical inference to long-term questions about human behavior, ecology, technology, and health.

Across projects in the Americas, Africa, Australia-linked collaborations, and comparative human evolution, the lab emphasizes explicit models, reproducible workflows, and careful treatment of uncertainty. The goal is not only to describe the past, but to test why patterns appear and how strong the evidence really is.

Core strengths: Bayesian inference, morphometrics, lithic analysis, zooarchaeology, human evolutionary ecology, machine learning for archaeological problems, and formal model building.
