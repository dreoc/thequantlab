Mathematical Models of Human Decision-Making
Erik Otárola-Castillo / November 2025 / Theory, Modeling

Our theoretical work integrates evolutionary ecology with computational modeling to understand human behavior across diverse environmental and cultural contexts. By formalizing anthropological theory into mathematical frameworks, we develop testable predictions about foraging decisions, technological innovation, and cultural transmission patterns.

This research bridges optimal foraging theory, life history models, and niche construction theory to explain how humans adapt to changing environments. We use agent-based models, dynamical systems, and Bayesian inference to test hypotheses about the evolutionary pressures that shaped human cognition and behavior.

Key Insight: Mathematical models reveal how energetic constraints, information availability, and social learning interact to produce the diverse patterns of human behavior we observe in the archaeological and ethnographic record.

Research Questions

Our theoretical framework addresses several fundamental questions:

  • How do humans make optimal decisions under uncertainty in variable environments?
  • What role does social learning play in the evolution of complex technologies?
  • How can we formalize cultural transmission as an evolutionary process?
  • What are the energetic trade-offs between different subsistence strategies?
Methodological Approach

We employ a multi-scale modeling approach that combines:

  • Optimization Models: Deriving analytically tractable predictions from first principles
  • Agent-Based Simulations: Exploring emergent properties of complex adaptive systems
  • Statistical Inference: Fitting models to archaeological and ethnographic data
  • Network Analysis: Mapping information flow and social learning networks

By integrating theory, simulation, and empirical analysis, we advance our understanding of human evolution and provide frameworks for interpreting the archaeological record. This work has implications for understanding contemporary human behavior and predicting responses to environmental change.

About This Work

Theoretical models provide the foundation for understanding human evolution by formalizing hypotheses into testable predictions.
