Erik Otárola-Castillo

PI profile
PI profile
Computational anthropology, archaeology, morphometrics, and Bayesian inference

Erik Otárola-Castillo is an Associate Professor in Purdue Anthropology. Public Purdue materials describe his work as centered on the question “What do people eat and why?” and emphasize the evolution, ecology, and diversity of behavior in prehistoric and modern hunter-gatherer populations.

His work also develops quantitative tools for archaeology and anthropology, including models for diet, space, time, and form, as well as morphometric and zooarchaeological workflows.

Research profile. Hunter-gatherer ecology, Paleoindian archaeology, Bayesian statistics, morphometrics, zooarchaeology, computational anthropology, and reproducible quantitative method.

Current lab-facing areas

  • Energetic and ecological modeling of forager behavior and diet.
  • Bayesian and likelihood-based inference in archaeology.
  • Morphometric approaches to lithics and bone-surface modifications.
  • Quantitative archaeology across the Americas, Africa, and comparative human evolution.
